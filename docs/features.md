## Multi-Year Analysis

Users can analyze all approved years together or switch into a single-year view. This supports both long-term trend evaluation and focused seasonal review.

## KPI Summary

The dashboard opens with a concise KPI strip that summarizes the most important metrics:

- Total tickets
- Total revenue
- Average ticket price
- Total orders
- Top ticket category
- Top purchase location
- Peak purchase hour
- Median days before event

## Purchase Timing

Timing views help answer when buyers purchase:

- Hour of day
- Day of week
- Days before event

These views can support campaign timing, reminder strategy, staffing, and pricing discussions.

## Deterministic Suggestions

The suggestion panel translates aggregate trends into practical prompts. Examples include flagging late-cycle purchasing, heavy manual-sales dependency, low self-service adoption, or audience concentration.

The system is intentionally rule-based rather than AI-generated so recommendations remain explainable and repeatable.

## CSV Ingestion And Admin Exploration

The private application supports approved preloaded data and an admin/local upload workflow for testing future years. In client mode, upload controls are hidden because browser-only uploads are not persistent.

The production-style handoff packages a static dashboard for internal hosting. End users can open the dashboard through a private URL without installing dependencies or handling raw CSVs.
